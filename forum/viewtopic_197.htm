OK understood, I will pay attention to that !

When you say "do not post commercial software titles" and "post the tinyurl only" you mean that we should NOT even mention their names in the forum but just refer to "target" right?

Also for those you may ask themselves why; this is my understanding of the why:

This is to avoid to be indexed by Google spiders. The primary tool that is used by software vendors to trace back "illegal" activity about their software is guess what ..... GOOGLE!
Avoiding to put the direct URL in this forum but rather using tinyurl which will play a URL hiding service in this particular case will prevent us to be exposed via Google indexation.
Also, we should avoid to mention the title-name of the software because that would also be indexed by Google and so we would be listed in such queries.

Now generalizing the concept .... if you are not sure of the legit aspect of the link that you're about to post; go for tinyurl ! We cannot be too cautious right?
Example that I personally experienced recently: we were discussing old but still excellent books (2002 and earlier and these are even difficult to buy nowadays) and I found an ebook version somewhere on the web .... Instead of using a direct URL to the ebook; I preferred to setup a quick tinyurl because I am pretty sure this ebook is not legit even if the book is an old one. In that was we should as well avoid to mention the title or maybe use a bitmap to show the title (maybe there is a plugin available somewhere to auto convert text into bitmap for this forum engine?)

Admin and Mods; feel free to correct me if I am wrong
I just wanted to provide some background because people comply better when they understand the "why" behind rules and here this is typical situation here where you have excellent reasons to setup some strong rules
