Re: Tutorial #10 is up

by random » Fri Jun 29, 2012 10:53 pm

Learn assembly if you don't know it.
Read other tutorials on easier stuff.
Find tutorials that have either crackmes or targets with them and try to crack them BEFORE reading the tut. Then, when you get stuck, start reading until you see what you did wrong, then try on your own again, only going to the tut when you have to.

The most important thing is to experiment. I learned more in my first all-nighter of trying to crack my own program than I did in months of reading tutorials and articles on cracking. I didn't even get close to cracking it, but I learned many things that can't be taught, one of the most important is getting a 'feel' for program flow.

It's really not that hard. Like anything, it just takes lots of practice.

And if all else fails, ask for help. There are people out there who would love to help you. If you get stuck, post the target and include where you're stuck. People love to help in this way.

Finally, if you truly want to know something forward and backward, write a tutorial on it- you can't fake it
